Shot Library

Tight

  • Tips: Keep the lead vocal framed tightly, but allow for some headroom.
  • Avoid: Cutting off the top of the head.

Medium

  • Tips: Keep the lead vocal framed tightly, but allow for some headroom.
  • Avoid: Trying to get two singers in the shot at once. This typically leaves a lot of empty space in the middle of the image.

Wide

  • Tips: Keep the lead vocal in the shot the whole time, but can walk the track.
  • Avoid: Allowing the lead vocal to leave the frame.

Walk

  • Tips: Change the speed you walk based on the tempo of the music. Pan from left to right and vice versa while you move for more energy.
  • Avoid: Jerky movements that cause the shot to become unusable.

Rack Focus

  • Tips: Rack focus on tight shots and wide shots.
  • Avoid: Doing shots like this during fast-paced sections. These shots tend to slow down the pacing.

Static

  • Tips: Use these for the sermon and other speaking moments.
  • Avoid: Unnecessary camera movements that can distract from the speaker.
