Collaborative Grids

Control It allows you to transform any local grid into a shared workspace stored safely in iCloud. Collaborate in real-time with your team, whether you are in the same room or across the globe.

Getting Started

To verify a grid is collaborative, look for the "Collaboration" status in your dashboard or the distinctive cloud icon on your grid tile.

Creating & Sharing

Making a Grid Collaborative

  1. Open any local grid's settings.
  2. Tap "Share Grid".
  3. This uploads your grid to iCloud and creates a secure link.

Inviting Others

Once shared, you can invite people via Messages, Mail, or by copying the link. You manage permissions (View Only vs. Can Make Changes) directly from the share sheet.

Syncing & Conflicts

Auto-Sync (Recommended)

By default, Auto Push and Auto Pull are enabled.

  • Changes you make are automatically uploaded.
  • Updates from others are automatically downloaded.

Manual Sync

If you prefer manual control, you can toggle these settings off in the Dashboard. Use the "Sync Now" button to manually check for updates.

Conflict Resolution

If two people change the same action at the same time, the latest change wins. Control It handles merging smartly, preserving unique changes to different parts of the grid.

History & Tracking

Tap "History" on the Dashboard to see a timeline of every change:

  • Who made the change?
  • What exactly changed? (e.g., "Updated HTTP Endpoint")
  • When did it happen?

This audit trail ensures you always know the state of your grid and can troubleshoot unexpected behavior.

Restoring Versions

If you need to revert to a previous state:

  1. Open the History view.
  2. Tap on any commit to view its details.
  3. Scroll to the bottom and tap "Restore this Version".

This will create a new commit that matches the state of the selected version, ensuring your history remains linear and safe.

Importing Grids

To join a shared grid:

  1. Copy the invitation link you received.
  2. Tap the "+" button to create a new grid.
  3. Scroll down to the "Join Existing" section.
  4. Paste the link and tap the arrow to import.

Leaving & Deleting

Leaving a Grid

You can leave a shared grid from the Settings menu. This removes it from your device but keeps it alive for other members.

Stopping Sharing (Owner Only)

If you are the owner, you can "Stop Sharing" to remove access for everyone and revert the grid to a private local copy.
